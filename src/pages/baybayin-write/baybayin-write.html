How it's Written

Baybayin is written based on how the word is pronounced.

Each consonant letter is a syllable that is pronounced with the A vowel. Each consonant is written as BA instead of B.

Vowels

Baybayin has 3 special vowel characters: A, I/E and O/U.

The vowels are used when a syllable doesn’t have a consonant.

A E O

(A) (E/I) (O/U)

Consonants

For traditional baybayin there are consonant called Final Consonants in which the consonant characters are not written.

For example in 'Bundok', NA and KA are not written because, ancient baybayin must contain a vowel sound, bundok is written as Bu Do.

Traditional:

bu do


Spanish Kudlit / Modernized:

bun+dok+

NGA Character

NGA is considered as a single character and it must be written that way.

N

(Nga)

Kudlit

It means a small cut or incision.

To change the sound of a character is changed by the position of the kudlit.

For I/Ethe kudlit is placed on top of the letter.

For O/Uit is placed below the letter.

The kudlit can be written or be in any shape. Usually it is written as a dot or tick but sometimes they are written/shaped like a V or >.

b be bo

(Ba) (Be/Bi) (Bo/Bu)

Spanish Kudlit / Francis Lopez Krus

This is to solve the Final Consonants problem, a Spanish Friar Fransisco Lopez invented a new kudlit (cross-kudlit) to cancel out the vowel sound.

Kudlit is used when a syllable doesn’t need a vowel sound.

However, there are Filipinos who didn’t accept the Spanish Kudlit because they were so used to the old writing style. Today, it has become popular among Filipinos who have rediscovered Baybayin.

b b+

(Ba) (B)

Vertical Bars

Vertical bars acted as punctuation to baybayin, it is merely used as a comma and/or a period. It can be written like || or | depending on the writer’s preference.

.

(period)
